EB-1 QUALIFICATION CRITERIA

USCIS Requires 3 of 10 Criteria.
Most Researchers Meet 5+ and Don't Know It.

The issue isn't whether you qualify. The issue is whether you can PROVE it. That's where most EB-1 cases fail‚Äînot qualification, but documentation. We know how to build evidence USCIS can't dispute.

1. Awards for Excellence

National/international prizes. Fellowship selections. Best paper awards at top conferences. Grant competitions you won. NOT participation certificates.

2. Membership in Associations

Selective membership requiring outstanding achievements. IEEE Senior Member. Exclusive professional societies. NOT associations anyone can join by paying dues.

3. Published Material About You

Media coverage in major outlets. Trade publications profiling your work. News articles about your research. NOT self-published content.

4. Judging Others' Work

Peer review for top journals. Grant panel reviewer. Conference program committee. PhD thesis examiner. Proof you're recognized as an expert by OTHER experts.

5. Original Scientific Contributions

Citations. Widely adopted methodologies. Patents commercialized. Research that shaped the field. THIS is where most cases succeed or fail.

6. Scholarly Articles

Publications in high-impact journals. Conference papers at top venues. Books published by academic presses. Quality matters more than quantity.

7. Critical Employment

Leading role at distinguished organization. Principal investigator on major grants. Department head. If your departure would significantly impact your organization, this qualifies.

8. High Salary

Compensation in top percentile for your field. Industry data comparison required. If you're paid like the top 10%, this helps. If not, focus elsewhere.
